Walt Disney

(1901–1966)

Here's a time line of Walt Disney:

  • 1901 - Disney was born on December 5 in Chicago, Illinois.

  • 1911 - His family moved to Kansas City, where Disney developed a love for trains.

  • 1917 - He dropped out of school to join the Army but was rejected for being underage. He was 16 years old

  • 1919 - Disney moved to Kansas City to pursue a career as a newspaper artist. His brother Roy got him a job at the Pesmen-Rubin Art Studio, where he met cartoonist Ubbe Eert Iwwerks. Disney worked at the Kansas City Film Ad Company, where he made commercials based on cutout animation.

  • 1922 - Disney and Iwerks started a small studio of their own and acquired a secondhand movie camera with which they made one and two-minute animated advertising films for distribution to local movie theatres. They also did a series of animated cartoon sketches and the pilot film for a series of seven-minute fairy tales that combined both live action and animation, Alice in Cartoonland.

  • 1923 - A New York film distributor cheated the young producers, and Disney was forced to file for bankruptcy. He moved to California to pursue a career as a cinematographer, but the surprise success of the first Alice film compelled Disney and his brother Roy—a lifelong business partner—to reopen shop in Hollywood. With Roy as business manager, Disney resumed the Alice series, persuading Iwerks to join him and assist with the drawing of the cartoons.

  • 1927 - Disney and Iwerks experimented with a new character—a cheerful, energetic, and mischievous mouse called Mickey.

  • 1928 - Disney recognized the possibilities for sound in animated-cartoon films and produced a Mickey Mouse cartoon film equipped with voices and music, entitled Steamboat Willie. It was a sensation.

  • 1933 - Disney produced a short, The Three Little Pigs , which arrived in the midst of the Great Depression and took the country by storm. Its treatment of the fairy tale of the little pig who works hard and builds his house of brick against the huffing and puffing of a threatening wolf suited the need for fortitude in the face of economic disaster.

  • 1934 - He began work on a version of the classic fairy tale Snow White and the Seven Dwarfs. Snow White (1937) was widely acclaimed by critics and audiences alike as an amusing and sentimental romance.

  • 1940s - Disney develop a wide variety of full-length entertainment films, such as Pinocchio (1940), Dumbo (1941), and Bambi (1942) etc.The Disney studio by that time was established as a big-business enterprise and began to produce a variety of entertainment films.

  • 1950s - Disney studio also began making full-length animation romances, such as Cinderella (1950), Alice in Wonderland (1951), and Peter Pan (1953), and produced low-budget, live-action films, including The Absent-Minded Professor (1961). In the other hand, Disney had initiated plans for a huge amusement park to be built near Los Angeles.

  • 1955 - Disneyland opened its doors, Disney’s disposition toward nostalgic sentiment and fantasy was evident in its design and construction. It soon became a mecca for tourists from around the world.

  • 1964 - The climax of Disney’s career as a producer came with his release of the motion picture Mary Poppins , which won worldwide popularity.

  • 1971 - A second Disney park was opened, Walt Disney World, near Orlando, Florida, which was under construction at the time of his death.
